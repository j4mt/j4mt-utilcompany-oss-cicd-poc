If your circumstances have changed and you're finding it hard to make payments, the best thing to do is to let us know as soon as possible. By putting it off you'll reduce your payment options, and be at risk of bigger charges and higher rates.

You can always contact us in confidence, and we'll do everything we can to help.

Payments received at least 6 working days before your next bill date will be credited to that bill. If the payments you made were:

  • More than your bill amount: any remaining credit will stay in your account and roll over to your next bill.
  • Less than your bill amount: you'll have to pay the remaining balance just like a normal bill.

Top-up payments will take up to 3 working days to show up in your online account, and you can make them using any of our available payment options.

Top 5 FAQs

Can I pay my bill in advance or make top up payment?

You can make extra payments at any time using any of our payment options, regardless of how you normally pay your bill.

If you don't have a current bill to pay, any payments you make will appear as credits in your account and will be used towards your next bill.

Why is my bill estimated?

Your meter is read by ESB Networks (electricity) or Bord Gais Networks (gas) four times a year, so outside of these times we rely on estimated readings to bill you.

If you recently submitted a meter reading your bill may still be estimated as we can only accept meter readings up to 5 days before the end of your billing period. These last 5 days may be estimated, or we may bill you to the date your reading was submitted ‚ this varies according to your billing dates and cycle.

Why are there two different rates on my bill?

You may have two different rates on your bill if any of the following happened during your billing period:

  • Your 12-month introductory period ended and you went onto our standard rates
  • Our rates changed (we'll always notify you first if this happens)
  • You changed your payment method to or from direct debit
  • You signed up to or cancelled e-Billing

Paying by direct debit and signing up to e-Billing will give you the lowest rates possible – you can add these discounts to your account at any time.

Can I get a credit balance refunded to my bank account?

If your account is in credit this usually means:

  • The payments you made during your billing period were more than your bill amount
  • You had a reversal that was larger than your current bill

We'll automatically roll your credit over to your next bill, but if the balance is significant you can contact our Customer Services team to arrange a refund.

I have a charge on my bill I don't understand

If you see a charge on your bill that you don't recognise, it will most likely be related to servicing or maintenance such as:

  • Reconnecting or disconnecting your meter
  • Special meter-read request
  • Locking your meter
  • Changing your meter type

These fees are charged by ESB Networks for electricity-related matters or Bord Gáis Networks for gas. Find full details on what other charges you might be billed for.
