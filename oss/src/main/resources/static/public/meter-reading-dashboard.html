Gas used

Your meter measures the volume of gas used in cubic meters (m3) or cubic feet (ft3). We use this measurement and a variable conversion factor to calculate the amount of energy you've used in kilowatt hours (kWh).
Conversion factor

Conversion factor

Your gas meter measures your gas consumption in cubic meters (m3) or cubic feet (ft3). To give you an idea of how much energy you use, we convert this to a standard kilowatt hour (kWh). The conversion factor is the number we use to do this calculation.

The conversion factor will change each month depending on industry gas prices, the gas composition, and the local temperature and pressure.
Energy usage

Energy usage

Your gas meter measures your gas consumption in cubic meters (m3) or cubic feet (ft3). To give you an idea of how much energy you use, we convert this to a standard kilowatt hour (kWh). To do this we take your previous reading from your current reading and apply a conversion factor which is affected by local temperature and pressure.

Top 5 FAQs

My meter box is locked, where do I get the key from?

If your meter box is locked, you can request a meter key online by sending us your email address and SSE Airtricity account number. Once we receive your details we'll send you a free meter key within 7 days.

Is it compulsory to send in meter readings?

It's not compulsory to send in meter readings, but your bill will be more accurate if you do (and it's extra important for gas customers).

Your electricity and/or gas meter is read 4 times a year, so we rely on estimates outside of these times to calculate your bill. By submitting regular meter readings you can minimise these estimates and make sure you only pay for what you use.

My meter reading was rejected. What does this mean?

If your meter reading was rejected, it's usually due to:

  • Inconsistencies with the readings e.g., for electricity Day and Night readings being in the wrong fields
  • A conflict with your meter type

This will come from either ESB Networks (electricity) or Gas Networks Ireland (gas) – we need to verify all your meter readings with them as they own and operate the meters in the Republic of Ireland.

What to do

If you check your latest bill you can see what range your meter readings should be in and what your MPRN/GPRN is. We also have a Meter Reading Guide with instructions on how to read the most common gas and electricity meters.

If you can't find any inconsistencies, try submitting a meter reading again and just contact our Customer Service team if you have any more issues. Remember it's important that you don't send us more than one reading on the same day, as this could cause your bill to be delayed.

Why did I get an estimated bill when I submitted a reading a few days ago?

If you received an estimated bill even though you just submitted a reading it usually means one of the following:

  • Your reading was rejected by the networks
  • Your reading was not received in time (we must receive it at least 2 days before the end of your billing period), or
  • We had to estimate the remaining days in your billing period.

If your reading was rejected you should look at our Meter Reading Guide to make sure you're reading your meter in the right way.

If your reading was accepted but your bill is still estimated, this is because we can only accept meter readings up to 2 days before the end of your billing period. We may sometimes estimate these remaining days – but this doesn't mean your whole bill is estimated.

How do I sign up for meter reading reminders?

You can sign up for meter reading reminders by:

  • Logging into My SSE Airtricity online and going to 'Meter Readings', or
  • Contacting our Customer Service team by phone or email.

You can choose whether to receive your reminders by text or email (or both) and they are customised to your billing period, so they'll be sent a few days before your bill is due.

How do I read my electricity meter?

Standard (24 hour) meter

Standard meters are the most common, and have one row of numbers.

To read your meter:

  1. Read the numbers from left to right, including any zeros.
  2. Ignore any numbers after the decimal point or in red. If your meter does not have a decimal point, read all numbers, including any zeros.

E.g. The reading for this meter would be 05812

Digital Standard meter

Digital meters have an electronic display which changes every 9 seconds. You may also have a ‘Scroll' button which allows you to manually change the screens.

To read your meter:

  1. Scroll or wait until your digital meter shows the following screen: E.g. The reading for this meter would be 0005812
  2. Write down the numbers from left to right, including any zeros.
  3. Ignore any numbers after the decimal point. If your meter does not have a decimal point, read all numbers, including any zeros.
