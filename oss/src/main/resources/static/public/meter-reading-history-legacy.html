Top 5 FAQs

How do I read a Standard or Wattless meter?

About your meter

Standard (24hr) and Wattless meters both have one row of numbers.

This row is labelled as either kWh or kVArh.

How to read your meter

  1. Read the numbers from left to right
  2. Ignore any numbers after the decimal point or in red
How do I read a Digital or Electronic meter?

About your meter

Digital Meters have an electronic display that changes every 9 seconds.

Some meter also have a "Scroll" button that allows you to manually change the screens.

How to read your meter

  1. Scroll or wait until your digital meter shows the screen on the right
  2. Write down the numbers from left to right
  3. Ignore any numbers after the decimal point

Remember:

When reading a digital meter, ignore the numbers after the decimal point.

What's a Meter multiplier?

If your premises uses more energy than can be displayed by your meter then your meter will show a fraction of the total energy used.

The meter multiplier allows us to calculate your actual usage.

So, if your premises used 100 kWh and has a meter multiplier of 3, your actual usage is 300 kWh

To calculate your electricity usage we take take your previous reading from your current reading, and then apply your meter multiplier value.
