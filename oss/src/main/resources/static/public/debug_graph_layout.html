Top 5 FAQs

How much of each monthly payment am I actually using?

How your monthly payment relates to your actual energy usage will vary depending on the time of the year.

For example, in the summer you will use relatively small amounts of energy so your payments will be higher than your actual usage. Since you're paying more than what you're using, you build up credit on your account.

On the other hand, during the winter you will use relatively large amounts of energy so your payments will be lower than your actual usage. During these months, your account will draw on the credit you built up during the summer.

On the back of your monthly statement there is a section called 'Usage Details' which details all your meter readings during this period. If you submit regular meter readings you can track your whole usage history online through My Airtricity.

How do I pay off an outstanding balance on my account?

If your account has an outstanding balance you don't necessarily need to pay it off. As you're paying the same amount each month, there are times of the year (i.e winter) when your payments will be less than the amount of energy you're actually using, so your account will be in debit. This evens out during the summer months, where your payments are typically higher than your energy costs.

We calculate your payments to cover the cost of your whole year's electricity and/or gas (and regularly review them), so making manual payments can lead to a build-up of credit on your account. If this happens, we won't be able to issue you a refund - instead your monthly payment amount will be reduced.

If you are worried about the outstanding balance on your account, you should contact our Customer Service team on 1850 81 22 20 to see if your monthly payments need to be increased.

Ways to keep your payments accurate

If you submit regular meter readings we will have accurate usage information to go by when we review your payments (after every four statements). You can also view a history of all your meter readings online through My Airtricity.

How are my monthly payments calculated?

We calculate your monthly payments using a number of factors:

  • Your current usage
  • Your previous usage
  • Time of year
  • Your rates

If we calculated the cost of your year's electricity to be €1,200, your fixed monthly payments would be €100. We will send you a letter stating the amount of your monthly payments and you'll receive your first Budget Plan statement within the next month.

Your first direct debit will be deducted approximately 14 days after your statement is issued. Please note your billing date may be brought forward if it's less than a month since your last bill.

Reviewing your monthly payments

We review your payments after every four statements. By submitting regular meter readings you can help make sure your monthly payments are in line with your usage.

If you start using more electricity (for example, if you build an extension to your house), you should contact our Customer Service team on 1850 81 22 20 to see if your monthly payments need to be increased.

When you start on Budget Plan you'll receive a letter stating the amount of your monthly payments.

You'll receive your first Budget Plan statement within the next month, and your first direct debit will be deducted approximately 14 days after your statement is issued. Please note your billing date may be brought forward if it's less than a month since your last bill.

Why have my monthly bills been recalculated?

We recalculate your monthly payments to prevent debit or large credit balances building up on your account. This happens after every four statements.

Credit or debit balances can occur when:

  • Your usage changes significantly (e.g., you built a house extension)
  • Your rates change, or
  • Your bill was estimated

We will recalculate your payments to reflect your most up-to-date usage, rates and meter readings, and if there is a change we will always notify you first by sending an email stating your new monthly payment amount.
