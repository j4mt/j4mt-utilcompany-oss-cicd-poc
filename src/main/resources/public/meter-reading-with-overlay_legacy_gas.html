How to read a Standard meter or Dial/Clock meter

How to read your Standard meter

Standard gas meters have one row and may have 3 or more numbers. The display may be either digital or have revolving numbers but you can read both in the same way.

To read your meter

  1. Write down the numbers from left to right
  2. Ignore any numbers after the comma or decimal point and any numbers in red

How to read your Dial/Clock meter

If you have a dial/clock meter it will have six dials. To take your meter reading, you'll need to read the four dials on the bottom row.

To read your meter

  1. Read the dials from left to right, ignoring any dials on the top row labelled as 'Cubic feet' or 'Each division: 10'.
  2. If the dial is between two numbers, always round down to the lowest number. If the dial is between 9 and 0, round down to 9.

    e.g., The reading for this meter is 2343
